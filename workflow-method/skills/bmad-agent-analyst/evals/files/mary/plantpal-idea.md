# PlantPal — Raw Idea Notes (for a Product Brief)

Founder's brain-dump, lightly cleaned up:

- I keep killing my houseplants. So do my friends. The info online is generic
  ("water when dry") and doesn't account for MY apartment — which window, how much
  light, that it's bone dry in winter from the radiator.
- Idea: a phone app where you take a photo of a plant, it identifies the species,
  and then it asks a couple quick questions about where it lives in your home and
  builds a personalized care schedule with reminders. When a plant looks sick, you
  snap another photo and it suggests what's wrong.
- The magic is the *personalization to the actual spot in your home*, not just
  generic species advice.
- Who: millennial/Gen-Z urban apartment renters with 3–15 houseplants who feel guilty
  about killing them. Not commercial growers, not serious botanists.
- Why now: plant-parent culture is big, phone cameras + vision models are good enough
  to ID species and spot common problems.
- Roughly aware of competitors (Planta, Picture This) but feels they're either generic
  schedulers or pure plant-ID with no real personalization or diagnosis loop.

## Things the founder has NOT decided
- Business model (subscription? one-time? freemium?) — genuinely unsure.
- Whether to build the species-ID model or use a third-party vision API.
- Android vs iOS first.
- Whether a social/community feature matters at all for v1.

## What the founder feels strongly about
- It must feel warm and encouraging, not naggy or shaming — these are people who
  already feel guilty.
- The personalization-to-your-home and the photo-based "what's wrong with my plant"
  diagnosis are the heart of it.
