# Market Brief (provided for research exercise)

**Product / sector:** A subscription meal-kit service focused on high-protein,
low-prep recipes aimed at time-poor fitness-oriented consumers in the United
States.

## Given data points (treat as the only established facts)

- The broad meal-kit category is widely described as crowded and mature, with
  several large incumbents and persistent churn; first-time-customer promotions
  are heavily used and retention past month three is repeatedly cited as the
  core problem.
- A small reader survey the founder ran (n = 220, self-selected, gym-app
  audience) found: 61% say "lack of time to cook" is their top barrier to eating
  well; 48% currently buy protein supplements; 27% had tried a generic meal kit
  before and 70% of those churned within 8 weeks, most often citing "recipes too
  fiddly / too much prep."
- Named adjacent/competing options the founder listed: large generic meal-kit
  incumbents, grocery-store prepared-meal aisles, restaurant/ghost-kitchen
  delivery, and DIY meal prep.
- Pricing intuition (not validated): the founder believes the target customer
  will tolerate a premium over generic kits if prep time drops below ~10 minutes
  per meal.
- Customer goals the founder believes matter: hitting a protein target, minimal
  prep/cleanup, predictable macros, and not having to plan.

## Notes / unknowns

- No third-party market-size figure, category CAGR, competitor revenue, or
  churn benchmark has been independently sourced — the only quantitative data is
  the founder's own small, self-selected survey above.
