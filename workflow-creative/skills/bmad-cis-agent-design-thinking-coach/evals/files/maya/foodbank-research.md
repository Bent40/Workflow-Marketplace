# Discovery notes — food bank first-time visitors (design-thinking input)

**Design challenge owner:** A regional food bank.

**Who:** First-time visitors to the food bank's distribution site. The visitor we
followed most closely is Ray, 41, recently laid off, who had never used a food
bank before and almost turned around in the parking lot.

**What we observed (notes from 5 ride-alongs + 6 short interviews):**

- Several first-timers circled the lot or sat in their car before coming in; two
  said they felt ashamed and weren't sure they "qualified."
- There's no clear front door or greeter — visitors guessed which line was for
  intake versus pickup.
- The intake form asks for proof of income; one visitor didn't have it and
  assumed they'd be turned away, so they left.
- Ray said: "I kept thinking everyone could tell it was my first time."
- A volunteer noted: "The ones who make it inside are usually fine. We lose the
  ones who never get out of the car."
- Regulars move fast and confidently, which makes first-timers feel more out of
  place.
- One interviewee: "I just wanted someone to tell me I was in the right place and
  it was okay to be here."

**Constraints:** volunteer-run, no budget for renovations, multilingual visitors,
high foot traffic on distribution days.

**What success would look like (owner's words):** more first-time visitors
actually coming inside and getting food, and feeling welcome rather than judged.
