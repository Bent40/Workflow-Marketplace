# GDD Excerpt — "Tideborn" (design facts for playtesting)

- **Genre:** 2.5D puzzle-platformer (Godot), single-player, PC + Switch.
- **Core pillars:** (1) "Momentum is a tool" — the player's speed solves puzzles; (2) "Read, don't memorize" — levels should be solvable by observation, not trial-and-error death.
- **Core loop:** enter a chamber → observe water-level + current → time a momentum-dash across moving platforms → reach the exit. ~2-4 minutes per chamber.
- **Tutorial:** the first three chambers teach dash, water-current riding, and the wall-bounce — WITHOUT any text prompts (the team is worried the no-text tutorial may be unclear).
- **Difficulty concern:** Chamber 7 ("The Gauntlet") chains three momentum mechanics; internal devs find it satisfying but suspect new players will wall here.
- **Open question this build must answer:** Do first-time players understand the dash + current mechanics from the no-text tutorial alone, and where do they get stuck before Chamber 7?
- **Build status:** a playable vertical-slice build (Chambers 1-7) is available. No telemetry/analytics is instrumented yet — observation and post-session interview only.
- **Audience:** target players are casual-to-mid puzzle-platformer fans; the team has 6 external testers lined up who have never seen the game.
