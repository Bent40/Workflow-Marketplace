# lit-teach — decision log

## Session 2026-07-06 (headless build, branch claude/literary-workflow-feedback-cdlwlt)

- **Origin**: explicitly user-requested ("create a teaching harness for
  aspiring writers... show examples and the way of thought the writer needs to
  tinker to reach this position").
- **Governance**: CREATE-NEW, project-specific (`lit-*` family). Key
  adjudication: ABSORBS the approved-but-unbuilt `lit-device-coach` — intent
  diagnosis ("what am I attempting / help me land this") is one intent of the
  teaching harness and both consume the R3 taxonomy; two skills firing on the
  same phrasing would violate the overlap rule. Pack candidate list updated
  accordingly. Neighbors checked: bmad-teach-me-testing (testing domain;
  precedent for progressive teaching, no overlap), bmad-cis-storytelling
  (creates narratives, doesn't answer craft questions), lit-line-edit
  (reviews; hands findings off to lit-teach for explanation).
- **Thin by design**: the knowledge base is the committed research corpus
  (docs/research/literary-pack/R1–R4) + _workflow/style-profiles/ — the skill
  ROUTES to sections on demand instead of duplicating content. Topics outside
  the corpus get live research with citation or an honest "not covered".
- **Core design**: the 7-step lesson shape whose center is step 4, "the
  writer's question" — the internal question that generates the move — because
  the user's requirement was the way of thought, not definitions. Intent-first
  diagnosis rule encodes R3's five signatures with the never-a-mere-error
  posture. Caveat step encodes R1's detector↔caveat pairs and voice-contract
  supremacy.
- **Fixtures**: eval prompts embed a ≤8-word manuscript excerpt ("Cael felt a
  warm streak of blood") per the user's explicit approval of corpus excerpts as
  eval fixtures; the near-chiasmus in lt-2 is constructed, not user prose.
- **Validation mode**: degraded in-session (trigger proxy + 4 simulated
  artifact evals); hermetic re-run via bmad-eval-runner when available.
- **Validation results (2026-07-06)**: trigger proxy 11/11 (all six teaching
  phrasings fire; teach-me-testing / prose-review / style-research / CI / git
  queries route elsewhere). Artifact evals 4/4 PASS — lt-1 finding-explain
  (lesson anchored to the flagged line; caught the POV-knowledge subtlety in
  de-filtering unprompted); lt-2 chiasmus intent-diagnosis ("three-quarters of
  a chiasmus", two completions differentiated by final-word emphasis, never
  framed as error); lt-3 voice-caveat (rule + protected device taught as one
  coherent distance-control system); lt-4 KB-routing (grepped corpus before
  teaching, taught from R2 ground truth, labeled constructed examples).
