# ParkPal — product brief excerpt (UX input)

**Product.** ParkPal is a mobile-first web app that helps city drivers find and
pay for street parking. Single surface for v1: responsive web (used primarily on
phones, occasionally on a tablet mounted in the car).

**Primary user.** Devin, a delivery driver who circles a block 3–4 times looking
for a legal spot while a customer's order goes cold. He needs to (1) see open
metered spots near a destination, (2) confirm a spot is legal right now (no
street-cleaning, no permit-only), and (3) pay and extend time without going back
to the meter.

**Stated needs (from discovery):**
- Map view showing nearby available metered spots, color-coded by availability.
- A spot-detail view with the live rules for that block (hours, rate,
  restrictions, street-cleaning schedule).
- One-tap "start session" and "extend time" with a saved payment method.
- A running timer with a warning before the session expires.
- Accessibility matters: drivers glance at this in bright sunlight, often
  one-handed, sometimes with the phone mounted at arm's length.

**Out of scope for v1:** garage/lot booking, multi-city, social features,
reservations.

**Brand note:** the founder said "calm, trustworthy, not flashy — like a transit
app, not a game." No specific colors, type, or component library chosen yet.
