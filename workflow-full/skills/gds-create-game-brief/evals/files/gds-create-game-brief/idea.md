# Game idea notes — "Tidewright" (raw brain dump)

A cozy-but-tense game where you run a lighthouse on a shifting coastline. By day you
repair and upgrade the lighthouse; by night you aim the beam to guide ships through
fog and rocks. If a ship wrecks, debris and survivors wash ashore and change the next
day (more repair work, new tools, new townsfolk).

Feel: calm competence under rising pressure. The fantasy is being the one steady light
keeping a small community alive against the sea.

Core loop (rough): day = manage/repair/upgrade the lighthouse and town; night = a
real-time beam-aiming + timing minigame guiding ships past hazards; outcomes feed back
into the next day.

Pillars I keep coming back to:
- The beam-aiming night phase has to feel tactile and tense, not idle.
- Day and night feed each other — last night's wrecks are today's problems.
- A small, named cast of townsfolk you actually get to know.

Audience: people who like cozy management/sim games (Stardew, Spiritfarer) but want
more moment-to-moment tension than a pure farming sim. Single-player, PC and Switch.
Short-to-medium sessions.

Comparables I think about: Spiritfarer (tone, named cast, day-to-day care) and
Papers, Please (a tense repeated minigame with moral weight). I want Spiritfarer's
warmth and Papers Please's pressure — but NOT Papers Please's bleakness or
Spiritfarer's slow pace.

Stuff I'm unsure about: how hard the night minigame should be; whether there's a
fail-state for the town or just a softer setback; budget and team (probably a 3-person
indie team, no firm timeline yet).

Not doing in v1: no multiplayer, no procedurally infinite map (the coastline is
hand-authored), no combat.
