# Tidewalker — game pitch (game-design input)

**One-line pitch:** A meditative tidal-flats exploration game where you walk an
ever-shifting shoreline, and the sea — not enemies — is your only real pressure.

**The fantasy the developer keeps describing:** "I want the player to feel the
patient, slightly anxious rhythm of beachcombing against a coming tide — the
quiet joy of finding something, and the gentle dread of the water rising while
you decide whether one more find is worth it."

**What we know so far (developer's notes):**
- 3D first-person, slow walking pace, no combat, no fail-state death — but the
  rising tide can strand you and reset you to the last dry ground (you lose
  uncollected finds, not progress).
- Core activity: explore the exposed flats at low tide, collect shells/artifacts,
  and read short flavor text about each. The map literally changes as the tide
  goes out and comes back in.
- The tide is on a visible, predictable cycle; tension comes from greed vs.
  caution, not twitch reflexes.
- Target player: people who like calm, atmospheric exploration games (Proteus,
  A Short Hike, walking sims) and want low-stress play with a little pull of
  risk.
- Mood: serene, lonely-but-not-sad, painterly.

**Platform / scope:** PC first, solo developer, ~6 month part-time scope.
Commercial-but-small ambitions.

**Explicitly undecided:** progression/upgrade systems, whether there's any
narrative beyond flavor text, monetization. The developer does NOT want these
assumed for them.
