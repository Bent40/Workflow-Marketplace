# Headless PRFAQ input — "ShiftSwap"

**customer (specific persona):** Maria, a 34-year-old shift-lead nurse at a 200-bed
regional hospital. She manages her own rotation plus covers the floor when
colleagues call out. She is not technical, works 12-hour shifts, and checks her
phone between rounds.

**problem (concrete):** When a nurse calls out sick, finding a qualified
replacement today means Maria phones or texts down a paper list one person at a
time, often after midnight, with no idea who is already over their hours or
unavailable. A single gap can take 90+ minutes of calls to fill, and roughly one
in six shifts goes uncovered, forcing the remaining staff to absorb unsafe
patient loads.

**stakes (why it matters):** Uncovered shifts directly degrade patient safety and
drive nurse burnout and turnover; the hospital pays premium agency rates
(2–3x base) for last-minute external fills, and Maria personally loses sleep and
goodwill chasing coverage that should not be her job.

**solution (concept):** A mobile app where an open shift is broadcast in seconds
only to nurses who are credentialed for that unit AND under their weekly hour cap;
eligible nurses see it on their phone, tap to claim, and the schedule updates
automatically with manager sign-off. ShiftSwap removes the midnight phone tree and
fills gaps from the qualified internal pool before any agency call is made.

**competitive context:** Hospitals currently use a mix of paper lists, group texts,
and heavyweight workforce-management suites (e.g. enterprise scheduling modules)
that admins find too slow and rigid for same-day swaps.

**team/org context:** Two-person founding team, one with nursing operations
background; targeting mid-size regional hospitals first.
