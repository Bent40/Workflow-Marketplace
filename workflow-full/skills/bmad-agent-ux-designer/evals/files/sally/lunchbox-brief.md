# LunchBox — product brief excerpt (UX input)

**Product.** LunchBox is a web app where office workers pre-order lunch from a
rotating set of nearby restaurants and pick it up from a labeled shelf in their
building lobby at a set time, skipping the line.

**Primary user.** Aisha, an analyst who gets 35 minutes for lunch and hates
gambling on whether a place will be slammed. She wants to (1) see today's menu
window and the pickup deadline, (2) order in under a minute, and (3) get a clear
"it's on the shelf" signal so she doesn't wander the lobby.

**Stated needs (from discovery):**
- A "today" view: which restaurant is on, the order-by time, and the pickup
  window.
- A fast item-select + checkout with a saved payment method.
- An order-status indicator: ordered → being prepared → on the shelf (slot
  number).
- A gentle reminder before the order-by cutoff.

**Surface:** responsive web, used mostly on desktop at a work computer, sometimes
on a phone walking to the lobby.

**Brand note:** "friendly and quick, like a good coffee shop — warm but not
cutesy." No colors, type, or component library chosen yet.

**Out of scope for v1:** group orders, dietary-filter search, multi-building,
loyalty points.
